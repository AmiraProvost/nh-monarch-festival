🚌 Getting There

Tioga Pavilion is located in Belmont Village in the heart of New Hampshire’s Lakes Region, easily accessible from I-93. The nearest major city is Laconia, NH, just minutes away.

From I-93 North or South: Take Exit 20 toward Laconia/Belmont. Follow Route 140 (Depot St) East to the stop sign, then take a right on Main Street. Mill Street is immediately to the right, next to the Belmont Public Library. The Tioga Pavilion will be on your right. We will have directional signs up.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the admission fee?

Ticket pricing will be announced closer to the event. Children under 2 are free. Visit our tickets page for the latest information.

Is the festival held rain or shine?

Yes! The NH Monarch Festival is a rain or shine event. We recommend bringing an umbrella or poncho just in case. In the event of severe weather, updates will be posted on our Facebook page.

Are pets allowed?

Out of consideration for the monarch butterflies and other wildlife at the festival, we ask that pets be left at home. Service animals are always welcome.

Is there food at the festival?

Yes! Food vendors will be on-site. We also encourage you to explore the great local dining options in and around Belmont — check out our About Belmont page for recommendations.

Can I become a vendor?

We welcome vendors whose products align with our monarch, milkweed, and pollinator theme. Visit our Vendor Application page to apply.

How can I help at the festival?

We’d love your help! Visit our Volunteer page to learn about opportunities and sign up.

I have a question not answered here. Who do I contact?

Please reach out through our Contact Us page and we’ll get back to you as soon as possible.
